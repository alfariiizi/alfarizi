# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Primary visitors are recruiters, hiring managers, and potential collaborators evaluating Moh Rizal Alfarizi through a personal portfolio site. They arrive to understand the quality of his judgment, the range of work he can own, and whether his way of working feels credible for serious product and engineering work.

## Product Purpose

This site presents selected writing, projects, and experience in a way that feels deliberate, calm, and technically grounded. Its job is not to exhaustively document every achievement, but to help the right reader quickly understand scope, taste, reliability, and the ability to ship useful software under real constraints.

## Brand Personality

Calm, sharp, convincing. The voice should feel mature, precise, and quietly confident, with enough warmth to remain personal without becoming chatty or performative.

## Anti-references

Do not make the site feel like a formal CV pasted onto the web. Avoid startup-style bravado, noisy badges, inflated claims, sentimental autobiography, and metric-heavy self-promotion that overwhelms the actual work.

## Design Principles

- Earn credibility through selection, not accumulation.
- Lead with judgment, scope, and outcomes rather than job titles.
- Keep the presentation composed and readable, even when the work itself is complex.
- Preserve a personal point of view without turning the site into a self-promotional performance.
- Let writing and structure do most of the persuasion, then use visual polish to support the message.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target a comfortable reading experience with readable contrast in both themes. Motion should stay restrained by default, respect `prefers-reduced-motion`, and never be required to understand the content.
